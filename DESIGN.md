# Design System: Therapist Resources Home Redesign V2
**Project ID:** 6540674488152958086

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
The atmosphere is **"Soft, Professional & Inviting."** It employs an airy, light, minimalist aesthetic explicitly tailored for therapeutic, educational, and wellness contexts. The design embraces abundant white space to feel uncluttered and approachable. It relies on a signature soft, ethereal gradient (transitioning from calming teal to gentle purple) that invokes a sense of tranquility, modern professionalism, and forward-thinking innovation.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- **Calming Teal (#38B2AC):** The primary brand color. Used for primary calls-to-action (CTAs), hover states, and iconography. It provides a soft, reassuring, yet vibrant focal point.
- **Gentle Lavender/Purple (#9F7AEA):** A secondary accent used primarily within gradients to add a touch of warmth and creativity without overpowering the soothing UI.
- **Signature Gradient (Teal to Purple):** A linear gradient combining the Teal and Lavender colors. Used extensively in the hero background, card top-borders, and decorative section dividers to create a cohesive brand feel. 
- **Pure White (#FFFFFF):** The structural canvas. Used for the main body background, card backgrounds, and input fields to ensure maximum text readability and airiness.
- **Deep Slate (#2D3748):** Used for all headers and primary body paragraph text. It provides excellent contrast and legibility without the harshness of purely pitch-black.
- **Muted Gray (#A0AEC0):** Used for subtle borders on inputs, secondary text, and social media icons in the footer.

## 3. Typography Rules
- The primary font family is **Inter** (a clean, modern sans-serif), ensuring maximum legibility across devices.
- **Headers & Display:** Use highly legible, bold weights (e.g., Medium to Bold) for headlines (e.g., "Modern Tools & Resources..."). Dark Slate color for strong hierarchy.
- **Body & Subtitles:** Standard weight (Regular), medium sizing with ample line height to prioritize comfortable reading for educational materials.
- **Buttons & Labels:** Medium weight for button text; small footprint but distinct to encourage interaction.

## 4. Component Stylings
* **Buttons (Primary):** Pill-shaped (fully rounded ends). Filled with either the Calming Teal color or the Signature Gradient, featuring white text. Finished with a very soft ambient shadow on hover.
* **Buttons (Secondary/Outline):** Pill-shaped transparent background with a thin Dark Slate or Gray border and dark text.
* **Cards/Containers:** Generously rounded corners (large border-radius). They sit upon the white background using medium-soft, diffused, wide-spread drop shadows to subtly "float" above the layout. Key cards (like the Newsletter signup) feature a prominent colored gradient top-border strip for visual anchoring.
* **Inputs/Forms:** Subtly rounded corners, pure white background, and a delicate gray outline border. Designed to be unobtrusive until focused.
* **Icons & Badges:** Small circular badges (often Teal-colored) that hold white minimalist icons; used to draw attention to features within cards.

## 5. Layout Principles
- **Grid Alignment:** Centered content with generous horizontal margins for max-width containment (constrained typically around 1200px width).
- **Whitespace Strategy:** Sections are divided by vast amounts of vertical whitespace (paddings of 80-120px). This "breathing room" prevents the page from feeling overcrowded or stressful, aligning with the therapeutic theme.
- **Asymmetry & Composition:** The hero section uses an asymmetrical split with a portrait cutout overlapping the background on the left and structured typography on the right, balancing visual human elements against textual information.
